Each genome has a neural network plus a small tail of physics/control trait genes. The network reads body state, target speed, balance, contacts, stance timers, foot velocities, previous motor commands and a weak evolvable clock signal, then outputs joint targets, lean and auxiliary swing/stiffness reflex commands. Trait genes tune clamped constants such as foot sweep, drive, drag, clock strength/rate and acceleration tolerance. The genetic algorithm keeps elites, breeds survivors and mutates them. A stagnation detector watches progress, diversity and behavior; when the population collapses into a local optimum it performs a partial restart with immigrants, hypermutants, mirror mutants and novelty-biased parents.

This is intentionally browser-friendly biomechanics, not medical-grade physics: it rewards plausible contact, balance, real foot-ground propulsion and low energy use while penalizing skating, hovering, one-leg dominance, glued-feet shuffling and tiny high-frequency foot-flick exploits.
